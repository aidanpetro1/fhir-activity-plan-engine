# Trisomy 21 Counseling Guidelines

Quick-reference anticipatory-guidance topics for Trisomy 21 (Down syndrome) health-supervision visits. Adapted from American Academy of Pediatrics guidance.

**Scope:** Discussion topics only. Screenings, labs, and referrals are tracked separately by the orderable schedules.

**Legend:** *(once)* = discuss at least once during this age range.

---

## Birth - 1 month

- Child's strengths and positive family experiences
- Down syndrome support groups and family resources
- Telling siblings, family, and friends; coping with disability
- Early intervention (PT, OT, speech); refer unless contraindicated
- Cervical spine precautions for anesthesia, surgery, or imaging
- Recurrence risk; offer genetic counseling referral
- Increased respiratory infection risk
- RSV prophylaxis if qualifying heart disease, airway issue, or prematurity
- Complementary/alternative treatments; discourage unsafe options

## 1 month - 1 year

- Early intervention progress; therapy at home
- Caregiver emotional status and family relationships
- Support resources: respite care, caregiver counseling
- Recurrence risk and prenatal testing; offer genetic counseling
- Complementary/alternative treatments

## 1 - 5 years

- PT, OT, speech therapy progress
- At 30 months: IFSP-to-IEP transition
- Healthy diet and activity; obesity prevention
- Reassure: delayed dental eruption, hypodontia
- Accurate anatomical terms; body autonomy and personal space
- Sexual exploitation risk: usually trusted people, not strangers
- Behavior, sibling adjustment, socialization, recreation
- Recurrence risk and prenatal testing options
- Complementary/alternative treatments

## 5 - 12 years

- School placement and progress; transition planning by ~age 10
- Elementary-to-middle-school transition; IEP work planning at 14
- Self-help skills, independence, hygiene, privacy
- Puberty prep (extra support); females: menses and dysmenorrhea
- When appropriate: contraception, STI prevention; advocate LARC
- Females: ~50% chance of a child with Down syndrome if pregnant
- Sexual exploitation risk
- Guardianship, supported decision-making, financial planning
- Avoid trampolines; contact sports risk spinal cord injury

## 12 - 21 years

- Transition to adult care: education, vocational, living options
- Guardianship vs. supported decision-making; ABLE Act planning
- Gynecologic care: menses, dysmenorrhea, suppression if needed; LARC
- Sexuality education: healthy development, boundaries, STI prevention
- Sexual exploitation risk
- Females: ~50% chance of a child with Down syndrome if pregnant
- Premature aging and increased Alzheimer disease risk
- Transfer to adult primary and subspecialty care
- Avoid trampolines; contact sports risk spinal cord injury
